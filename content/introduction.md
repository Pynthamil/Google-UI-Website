Google is *just a girl* — wildly ambitious, chronically experimental, and incapable of committing to half the things it creates. Over the years, it has launched products with breathtaking confidence, only to quietly escort many of them to an early grave. Entire ecosystems have appeared, flourished, and vanished faster than most of us can emotionally process a cancelled favorite app.

As someone with far too much curiosity and a suspicious amount of free time, I started wondering: is this chaos random, or is there a hidden pattern behind Google’s product graveyard?

So I turned to data.

Using a Kaggle dataset tracking Google’s discontinued and active products, I set out to map the rhythm of Google’s ecosystem — its bursts of expansion, its waves of extinction, and the lifespan of the ideas caught in between.

Bring flowers if you must. We’re about to visit a graveyard.

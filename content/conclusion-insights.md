Google’s shutdowns are not random accidents. They follow cycles of expansion, overcrowding, and intentional cleanup.

The ecosystem grows quickly, fills itself with ideas, and then clears space for the next wave of innovation.

The graveyard is not just a record of failure. It is a record of movement.

Google survives not because its products last forever — but because it is built to evolve.

**Permanence is optional. Evolution is mandatory.**
